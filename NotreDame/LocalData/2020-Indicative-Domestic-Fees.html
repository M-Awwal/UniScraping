2020 INDICATIVE ANNUAL DOMESTIC FEES

At The University of Notre Dame Australia your tuition fees vary depending upon your program and the courses you take within that program. All courses are allocated a study load and a discipline area. The fees shown in this document are indicative of a full-time study load in the specified discipline areas. Fees will vary based on the number and type of courses chosen. Programs that have an overload component will attract higher annual fees. For assistance please contact the Student Fees Office: student.fees@nd.edu.au

The tuition rates in this document are the indicative annual rates that apply to study undertaken in 2020.

HOW FEES ARE CALCULATED

Fees are calculated based on the discipline and study load of the courses undertaken by students. For the majority of programs, courses studied do not all fall within the same discipline. For example, if you are enrolled in a Bachelor of Education, you may undertake some courses that are classified as ‘education’ and some that are classified as ‘humanities’. In this example your student contribution for the humanities courses will be calculated against the Humanities discipline, while the education courses will be calculated against the Education discipline.

To access individual course costs please visit the University’s Fees webpage: notredame.edu.au/study/fees

PRE-UNIVERSITY PROGRAMS

Students undertaking the Tertiary Pathway Program (TPP) and who are awarded a Commonwealth Supported Place will pay no fees on submission of a HECS-HELP form. Students awarded a Fee Paying place may pay upfront or if eligible access FEE-HELP.

HOW WILL I PAY?

Students who are Australian Citizens, holders of Permanent Humanitarian Visas and eligible New Zealand Citizens who wish to defer their fees, have access to the Australian Government’s HELP loan schemes HECS-HELP and FEE-HELP. The HECS-HELP loan scheme assists eligible students, who have been allocated a Commonwealth Supported Place, in paying their tuition fees. The FEE-HELP loan scheme assists eligible students who have been allocated a Fee Paying Place, in paying their tuition fees. Repayments in both schemes can be deferred until students are in the workforce. Students should consult www.studyassist.gov.au for further information and up to date eligibility requirements.

These fees are indicative and may vary with the mix of courses selected. Fee levels vary from year to year and the University makes no guarantee that the current fee levels will be maintained in future.

Students who elect to take up the FEE-HELP loan scheme are required to pay back the cost of their courses of study plus indexation in line with the CPI. From Semester 1, 2019, commencing students studying

an undergraduate program at the University are no longer charged the 25% FEE-HELP loan fee.
